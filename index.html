Ito's Integral, Black-Scholes Model, and Beta Hedging

▸ Project Description

Multi-part project completed as the midsemester project for my second semester mathematical finance course. The first part of the project involved the calculation, simulation, and comparison of an Ito's integral and a Stratonovich itegral, and then simulating hypothetical stock prices using the Black-Scholes model. The second part involved running 1000 simulations of a hypothetical stock and then comparing the theoretical and realized variance of the stock. The final part involved collecting real world S&P 500 options data and attempt to reduce the vega exposure for options alpha and beta in a simulated beta hedging scenario using the Black-Scholes formula.
